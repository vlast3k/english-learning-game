# Mirror Hall Background Prompt

Purpose: Slice 4's one-room light puzzle. Alex must see a daylight source, a fixed mirror, two movable mirror mounts, and a sealed sun-panel destination in one readable composition.

## Image Prompt

Use case: illustration-story.
Asset type: 16:9 painted Phaser point-and-click adventure background.

Create a warm, kid-friendly hand-painted inner jungle temple hall in the established Sun Temple Adventure storybook style. This is a broad, calm Mirror Hall, not a maze and not a dark cave. One fixed camera must show the entire visual reading order in a single composition: high daylight opening, fixed wall mirror, nearer movable mirror mount, farther movable mirror mount, sealed sun-panel recess on the far wall, then one small high architectural slit suggesting a route upward.

Composition and playability:

- 16:9 wide landscape background; lower third is quiet, open, safe ground for character walking;
- upper corners remain calm for HUD; upper middle has space for dialogue;
- place the high daylight opening at upper left or upper centre with a small natural warm glow only;
- place a fixed mirror set into the wall directly downstream of the opening; it must look permanent and non-movable;
- place two clearly different, clean, unoccupied movable-mirror mounting areas across the middle ground: a near mount and a far mount, each with a simple sun-marked pivot base and generous plain stone around its future overlay footprint;
- place one large circular sealed sun-panel recess on the far wall, visibly downstream of the far mount, with an empty clean central space for later open-panel and Mirror Lens overlays;
- place one small high architectural outlet beyond or above the panel for a later non-clickable upward glint;
- use gentle warm honey stone and muted teal jungle foliage only at the room edges; calm welcoming daylight, no dramatic darkness.

Critical state-art constraints:

- do not paint any beam, ray, laser, sparkly route, or line connecting the objects; transparent state overlays will supply every beam path;
- do not paint movable mirrors, mirror orientations, a Mirror Lens, an open panel, an inventory item, a map marker, UI, character, NPC, text, arrows, labels, numbers, or symbols that resemble writing;
- do not fill the mirror mount footprints with vines, rubble, wall texture, shadows, plants, or reflections;
- do not make the floor glossy or reflective, because it would compete with the later beam overlays.

Avoid:

- extra mirrors, mirror shards, reflective pools, treasure chests, keys, pedestals, books, ropes, jars, cups, loose lenses, coins, gems, tools, statues, or extra doors;
- skulls, weapons, traps, black voids, hostile faces, horror, danger, a threatening ruin, readable inscriptions, graphic arrows, watermark, borders, or franchise references.

## Overlay Notes

The separate Slice 4 state atlas supplies two states for each movable mirror, all beam segments, sealed/open panel states, the Mirror Lens, Compass lens overlay, and the future locked Observatory Approach map marker. Keep all mounts, panel recess, and beam-path surfaces clean enough to accept those overlays without visible rectangular cutouts.
