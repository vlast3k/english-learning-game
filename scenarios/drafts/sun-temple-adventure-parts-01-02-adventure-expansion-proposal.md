# Sun Temple Adventure: Parts 1 And 2 Adventure Expansion Proposal

## Purpose

This proposal strengthens the Green Lens and Blue Lens chapters without changing the campaign's peaceful, child-friendly identity.

It responds to one central problem in the current walkthrough: the player can complete the later lens chains correctly without feeling that they have uncovered anything important. The route currently proves that Alex can collect and use objects. It does not yet make the player ask, **what is this valley, and why do these small actions matter?**

The recommendation is not to add more locks, errands, or lore text. Instead, each chapter should contain a compact adventure arc across five or six existing screens:

```text
notice a mystery -> follow physical evidence -> help or repair something
-> uncover a hidden purpose -> make a visible change -> receive a new question
```

The existing geography already supports that shape. The first pass should enrich the current screens before any new maps are commissioned.

## The Story Truth The Player Gradually Learns

Mira does not know this at the opening, and the player should not receive it as an explanation. It is the designer's underlying truth:

> The Sun Compass is not a treasure detector. It is a small part of the valley's old light-and-water system. Its lenses reveal broken routes, and the Sun Temple once guided morning light through those routes to keep the valley's gardens alive.

The immediate flower at Base Camp is therefore only the first visible symptom. By the end of Part 2, the player should have enough evidence to believe that the whole location is affected:

| Evidence | What the child can infer |
| --- | --- |
| The camp flower no longer faces the temple | Something is wrong with the valley's light. |
| Lina's beds are dry and her seeds cannot be planted safely | The problem affects people and living things, not only a decorative flower. |
| Sun marks recur on the path, bridge, cave, and Compass | These places belong to one old system. |
| The Green Lens reveals a water route | The Compass reveals connections rather than simply unlocking levels. |
| Cave light exposes an old record of water and reflected light | The temple was built to carry something useful through the valley. |
| The Blue Lens connects the cave and river routes on the Compass | The separate places form one larger network. |

None of this needs a history lesson. The world should show one piece, then allow Alex and Mira to form a short, incomplete theory.

## Chapter Rules

1. Every screen answers one small question and creates the next one.
2. Every chapter has one human or living-world stake, one physical obstacle, and one discovery that changes the campaign question.
3. Looking should reward curiosity with evidence, not merely reveal a collectible.
4. A required interaction may be simple, but its result must be visible and persistent.
5. Do not add a new mandatory inventory object unless it changes the player's understanding of a place as well as opening a route.
6. Keep mandatory dialogue to one idea at a time. Optional observations carry the extra texture.

## Part 1 — The Garden That Forgot Morning

### Chapter promise

Alex follows a broken map route to help Lina protect her seeds, then discovers that the first lens belongs to a much older network that once connected the garden to the waterfall.

### What changes from the current version

The mechanical backbone remains: map -> rope -> bridge -> basket -> Lina -> Green Lens. The improvement is that each location contributes a clue to one coherent question: **why are the garden and camp both losing their light?**

### Six-screen adventure arc

| Screen | The player notices / does | What it reveals | Persistent or visible response |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Base Camp: a flower facing the wrong way** | Alex notices the drooping sun flower and the Compass's empty lenses. A faint line on the torn map stops before the river. | The missing light is a valley problem, and the Compass may be related; neither Mira nor Alex knows how yet. | The flower remains drooping. The torn map has a visible broken light-route line, not just a generic tear. |
| **2. Supply Tent: expedition evidence** | Alex takes the rope, but can also inspect a weathered route tag or rolled field sketch near it. | Mira's previous expedition followed sun marks toward the river; rope was packed for the old bridge. This makes the rope a deliberate expedition tool, not loot. | Optional sketch shows the same sun-and-river symbol seen at camp. No new item is needed. |
| **3. Camp Edge: recover the missing route** | A paper corner and a sun mark are visible beneath the branch. Alex moves it, takes the piece, and joins the map. | The missing map section contains the river route and a small mark beside Lina's garden. Alex has restored a piece of knowledge, not only opened a menu. | The combined map visibly joins a line from camp to river. A new small garden/river symbol becomes legible. |
| **4. Jungle Path: follow the fading markers** | Alex investigates one or two old sun stones. One is covered in moss; another catches a weak glint when inspected. | The route was intentionally marked. The old system reached beyond the temple, but the markers are no longer bright. | The bridge direction is framed by the final marker, creating a visual destination rather than a menu exit. |
| **5. Broken Bridge: the interrupted connection** | Alex sees Lina's basket on the far side and an old sun mark carved into the bridge post. Alex ties the rope and crosses. | The bridge carried people and supplies between the garden and the river. The basket makes the consequence personal: Lina's seeds are stranded while the beds are drying. | The rope stays tied. Crossing reveals a brief view of the greener river side, contrasting with the tired garden beds. |
| **6. Village Garden: a living clue** | Lina explains why the basket matters, receives it, and begins setting seed trays beside the flower pot. Alex notices that the seedlings lean toward a narrow gap in the trees—the temple direction. | The garden once received morning light from that direction. Lina found the Green Lens beneath the pot because it glowed when the garden still had light; she saved it without knowing its purpose. | Returning the basket changes the garden from abandoned to active: seed trays appear, Lina works, and the Green Lens is visibly revealed in the flower pot. |

### The Green Lens should be a discovery, not a payment

The lens can still be under the flower pot, but Lina should not say only, “Here is your reward.” The player earns the right to understand its context:

```text
Lina: My seeds are safe. Look—the little plants still turn toward the temple.
Alex: Like the flower at camp.
Lina: I kept this bright glass under the pot. It used to shine at sunrise.
```

The lens appears after this exchange. The object has a local history and a reason to be there, while the interaction remains simple.

### End-of-chapter revelation: the Compass is mapping a system

On placing the Green Lens, the Compass should animate a green thread from the garden/river symbol to a new Waterfall Cave marker. It should not merely make a marker appear.

```text
Mira: It found an old route beside the water.
Alex: The Compass is rebuilding the valley map.
```

Optional map detail: the new route has a tiny dark interruption at the cave. That creates Part 2's question before anybody says “find a light.”

### What the player has learned by the end of Part 1

- The garden's trouble and the camp flower's trouble are related.
- The valley once had marked routes between its people, water, and temple.
- The Green Lens reacts to that route.
- The Compass reveals where the system is broken next.

## Part 2 — The Cave That Kept the Light

### Chapter promise

Alex follows the route revealed by the Green Lens, learns to carry living light into a dark place, and discovers that the cave was not a hiding place for treasure: it was a small service room in the valley's forgotten light system.

### What changes from the current version

The mechanical backbone remains: jar + glow leaf -> light cave -> key -> blue box -> Blue Lens. The richer version gives each existing screen a purpose in an investigation. The player begins by asking **why does the new route disappear into darkness?** and ends by asking **how can the temple carry light when there is no sun inside it?**

### Five-screen adventure arc

| Screen | The player notices / does | What it reveals | Persistent or visible response |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Base Camp: the route has a blind spot** | After Green Lens placement, the Compass shows the new water route, but its last section is dark. Mira has no answer; Alex points out that the line stops at the waterfall. | A lens does not solve the valley by itself. It leads Alex to a place where understanding is missing. | The map's green route ends in a soft dark patch at Waterfall Cave. |
| **2. Keeper Hut: tools from a living place** | Alex finds the empty jar and inspects Lina's shade plants / a simple leaf chart. | Lina's gardeners use jars to protect tiny glow leaves after dusk. This grounds the future glow jar in local knowledge rather than a convenient crafting recipe. | The shelf includes one closed shade plant and a small sun-marked watering dish; both are optional observations, not new interactions. |
| **3. Waterfall Mouth: a bright place with a dark secret** | The waterfall is lively, but the cave behind it is unnaturally dark. Alex finds a glow leaf and notices sun-shaped stone grooves carrying water toward the cave wall. | Water and light meet here. The cave was built into the route, not accidentally found behind a waterfall. | Once the glow jar is used, warm points of light appear along the entrance grooves and remain lit. |
| **4. Dark Cave: read the room with light** | The jar reveals more than a key: a wall mural or three simple carved pictures—waterfall, a beam entering a temple, plants turning upward. A blue glint marks the seam holding the key. | The ancient builders used the cave to check or guide the route. The key is a meaningful piece of that maintenance system, not an arbitrary hidden object. | Light reveals the mural and blue sun mark. The key becomes visible only after the player can see this evidence. |
| **5. Blue Stone Box: an old message continues** | Alex opens the blue box. Inside, the Blue Lens rests on a shallow dish beside a simple route tile showing cave -> river -> temple. | The Blue Lens is a working part stored by a keeper, not buried treasure. The old system sent water and light onward to the temple. | The open box emits a calm blue reflection toward the cave mural; its solved state remains. |

The return to **Base Camp** is the chapter coda rather than a sixth new area. This keeps the current geography while giving the chapter a final meaning-making beat.

### The cave mural must be readable before it is explained

Use three clear pictures, no paragraph of temple lore:

```text
[waterfall] -> [light line] -> [three plants facing upward]
```

Alex can say, after looking:

> Water, light... and the valley plants.

Mira should only add a partial theory after the Blue Lens is placed:

> The cave route and the river route are part of one system.

This lets the child notice the connection before a character names it.

### The Blue Lens placement should deepen the mystery

The current “Temple Steps are on the map” is a functional hook, but not a chapter ending. Replace it with an effect that combines the Green and Blue routes on the Compass: green identifies the living water route; blue outlines a path from darkness toward the temple.

```text
Alex: The cave route joins the river route here.
Mira: Then the temple did not keep light for itself.
Alex: It sent it somewhere.
```

The Temple Steps marker then appears where those lines meet. In the same moment, make a small remote consequence visible: Lina's garden marker gains a faint warm edge or the Base Camp flower lifts its head a little, then settles. It is not healed; it is responding. The player now has evidence that repairing the Compass can affect the wider valley.

### What the player has learned by the end of Part 2

- The Waterfall Cave is a purposeful part of the old system.
- Water, living light, gardens, and the temple belong to the same story.
- The lenses are not prizes; they reactivate the Compass's ability to reveal connections.
- The temple's light must travel onward, so Temple Steps is an investigation with stakes, not just the next map button.

## Optional Texture: Things Worth Looking At

These observations make a screen feel inhabited without creating false puzzle objects. Each should be one short optional line and one visual detail.

| Location | Optional visual / observation | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Base Camp | Field sketch with a bright temple-to-garden line crossed out at the end | Seeds the campaign question. |
| Supply Tent | Coiled rope tagged `River bridge` | Makes the tool feel planned and real. |
| Jungle Path | Moss-covered sun stone with a tiny bright edge | Creates a trail worth following. |
| Broken Bridge | Bridge post carved with the same sun-and-river mark | Connects human infrastructure to the old route. |
| Village Garden | Seedlings leaning toward the distant temple gap | Gives Lina's scene a living visual clue. |
| Keeper Hut | Shade plant closed tight beside a sun-marked dish | Establishes light as part of local ecology. |
| Waterfall Mouth | Grooves that guide water toward the cave and glow after the jar is used | Makes the waterfall location purposeful. |
| Dark Cave | Three-picture mural and blue glint | Gives the key discovery a story reason. |

## Scope Boundaries

This proposal is intentionally richer in meaning, not larger in mechanics.

- Keep the current required item count and the existing map structure.
- Do not add a second NPC, a villain, timed danger, combat, a journal-reading gate, or a grammar gate.
- Do not make the mural a code puzzle. It is evidence, not homework.
- Do not require the player to inspect every optional detail to progress.
- Do not add a new screen in the first pass. If later playtests still feel rushed, the best candidate is a small behind-the-waterfall antechamber between Waterfall Mouth and Dark Cave—not an additional fetch location.

## Recommended Delivery Order

1. Add the Part 1 visual evidence and revised Lens-placement reveal; do not alter its successful mechanical route.
2. Add Part 2's cave mural, entrance-groove light state, and stronger Blue Lens coda.
3. Test with children for two specific outcomes: whether they can say what the Compass does, and whether they expect fixing the temple to help the valley.
4. Only then decide whether either chapter needs an additional screen or a larger puzzle.

## Success Criteria

After Part 2, a child should be able to say a version of:

> The Compass shows the old paths. The water, the light, and the plants are connected. We need to fix the temple so the light can reach the valley again.

If the child only says, “I found a green lens and then a blue lens,” the chapter still needs more visible cause and effect.
