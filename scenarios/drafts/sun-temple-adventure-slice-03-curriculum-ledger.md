# Sun Temple Adventure Slice 03 Curriculum Ledger

This ledger checks Temple Steps language against `curriculum-scope.md`. The slice remains adventure-first: no grammar answer gates.

## Current Status

Status: implemented as adventure vocabulary with Bulgarian support and no grammar-question progression.

## Language Policy

Use short, causal lines that name what the child can see and do:

- `This cup is empty.`
- `The waterfall has water.`
- `The gate needs water.`
- `These steps are old.`
- `The water opens the flower.`
- `I can see the Stone Lens.`

Avoid history exposition, passive constructions, future tense, and abstract words such as `ceremony`, `channel system`, `activate`, or `sacred`.

## Grammar Coverage

| Construct | Slice-three use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `be` | cup/gate/flower states | `This cup is empty.` |
| `have/has` | waterfall property | `The waterfall has water.` |
| `this/these` | cup and steps | `This cup is empty.` `These steps are old.` |
| simple present, third-person `-s` | visible cause | `The water opens the flower.` |
| `can` | reward comprehension | `I can see the Stone Lens.` |
| prepositions | lens location | `The lens is in the flower.` |
| commands | UI action | `Use the cup on the water.` |

This slice adds demonstratives and a third-person simple-present form naturally. Later slices should still cover explicit `do/does` questions, present continuous, and adverbs of frequency through story rather than quiz gates.
