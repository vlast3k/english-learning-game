# Level 10 Design: Earth Helper Mission

## Goal

Agent Alex reaches an outdoor Earth helper station near a park path. The final campaign gate opens only after Alex helps sort recycling, choose items to reuse, and explain what the helper team is doing.

## Curriculum Focus

- Main grammar: `can` / `can't` for helpful Earth actions.
- Main grammar: present continuous questions, especially `What are they doing?` and `Are they ...?`.
- Vocabulary: recycling bin, reuse, trash, seed, park, Earth, bottle, paper, clean up, pick up, plant, build.
- Reading pocket: a short helper board note about children cleaning the park, reusing paper, and planting seeds.

## Characters

- Agent Alex: the child hero.
- Ranger Nia: a calm Earth helper guide who checks the final action sentences.

## Items And Puzzle Beats

- `recycling_bin`: A sorting bin for bottles and paper. It practices `can`, `recycling bin`, `trash`, and `pick up`.
- `reuse_box`: A helper box for bottles and paper. It practices `reuse`, `can't throw away`, and `build`.
- `seed_packet`: A packet for planting near the park path. It practices present continuous questions with `plant`, `clean up`, and `doing`.

## Puzzle Flow

1. Read the Earth helper board.
2. Collect the recycling bin clue and answer a can/can't sorting challenge.
3. Collect the reuse box and answer a reuse/build challenge.
4. Collect the seed packet and answer present continuous action questions.
5. Talk to Ranger Nia for the Earth helper check.
6. Ranger Nia opens the Earth gate.
7. Pass the exit gate review quiz.
8. Exit to complete campaign Levels 01 to 10.

## Believability Notes

- The recycling bin matters because trash on the path must be sorted before the team can leave the park clean.
- The reuse box matters because useful paper and bottles should become helper tools instead of trash.
- The seed packet matters because the team is planting seeds after cleaning the park.
- Ranger Nia opens the gate only when Alex can say which actions the team can do and what they are doing now.
