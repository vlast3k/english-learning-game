# Level 06 Design: River And Mountain

## Goal

Agent Alex reaches an outdoor route station where a river, a village road, and a mountain path meet. The child must read the route board, collect three travel clues, answer place questions, and ask the guide where the safe path is before crossing to the next mission.

## Curriculum Focus

- Main grammar: `Where is...?`, `Where are...?`, and answers with place phrases.
- Review grammar: `There is/There are`, `This is/These are`.
- Vocabulary: river, mountain, boat, bridge, map, photo, city, country, village, road, path, left, right.
- Reading pocket: a short route-board note comparing city, country, and village paths.

## Characters

- Agent Alex: the child hero.
- Guide Mira: a friendly route scout who checks that Alex can describe where the travel clues are.

## Items And Puzzle Beats

- `river_map`: A map beside the route board. It practices `Where is the river?` and `The river is on the map.`
- `boat_ticket`: A small boat ticket near the river dock. It practices `Where is the boat?` and `The boat is next to the bridge.`
- `mountain_photo`: A photo of the mountain path. It practices `Where are the mountains?` and `They are behind the village.`

## Puzzle Flow

1. Read the route board mission plan.
2. Find the river map and answer a word/grammar challenge.
3. Find the boat ticket and answer a transport/place challenge.
4. Find the mountain photo and answer a place/compare challenge.
5. Talk to Guide Mira. She asks where the river and mountains are.
6. Guide Mira opens the mountain path.
7. Pass the exit gate review quiz.
8. Exit to the next campaign step.

## Believability Notes

- The map is needed because the route station has three possible paths.
- The boat ticket is needed because the bridge area is by the river crossing.
- The mountain photo is needed because the safe mountain path is visually marked behind the village.
- Guide Mira only opens the path after Alex proves they can explain the route.
