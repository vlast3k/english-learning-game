## Strongest Elements

- The core chain `jar + glow leaf -> glow jar -> lit cave -> key -> box -> Blue Lens` reads like classic point-and-click logic, not a quiz wrapper.
- Prop economy is disciplined: every major object has a job, state, and screen purpose.
- Child-safe tone is strong: glow jar instead of torch, calm cave instead of danger, no rival, no traps, no scary cave dressing.
- The Green Lens revealing Waterfall Cave continues the promise that the world changes because the child solved something.
- The three screen identities are clear: practical hut, bright waterfall gate, calm blue cave reward room.

## Risks And Design Gaps

- The motivation for visiting the keeper hut is thin unless Mira/Lina or visual staging plants the jar idea.
- The stone key in the wall risks feeling arbitrary without a fair visual clue.
- The cave interior has two competing focal points: wall crack/key and blue stone box/lens.
- `glow_leaf` could read as a magic collectible rather than a combine part if it looks too gem-like.
- The Blue Lens could disappear visually in a blue-lit cave.
- Some draft English lines are functional but not warm.

## Required Improvements

- Add setup line: `A jar can hold light.`
- Give the wall crack a fair clue: tiny sun mark, blue glint, or water trickle pointing to the crack.
- Stage the dark cave as a left-to-right mini-scene: cave opening/glow source, wall crack clue, blue stone box final focus.
- Make the keeper-hut jar singular and unmistakable.
- Place the glow leaf near the cave mouth, but not inside it.
- Make the Blue Lens distinct from the blue cave and box with a clear rim, sun motif, or brighter silhouette.
- Keep the glow jar visually meaningful after use by matching the cave-lit overlay color.

## Questions Answered For The Team

- **How does the child know to look for a jar?** Mira/Lina gives the simple clue: `A jar can hold light.`
- **What visual clue makes the wall crack fair?** A tiny blue glint and small sun mark point to the crack.
- **How is the Blue Lens distinct from the blue cave?** It has a bright cyan rim, simple sun motif, and cleaner circular silhouette than cave reflections.
- **What is the dominant focus of the dark cave before the box opens?** The shut blue stone box is the main focus; the wall crack is a secondary clue area.
- **Does the waterfall communicate blocked entrance first?** Yes: the cave entrance is the dominant dark shape; the waterfall frames it but does not hide it.
- **Can hut objects outshine the jar?** No: no extra jars, lamps, tools, or glass shapes are allowed.
- **What is the slice's memorable aha?** The child makes a glow jar from a hut jar and a waterfall leaf, then uses it to light the cave.

## Recommendation

Conditional go became **GO for prompt generation** after the required improvements above were integrated into the docs and prompts.
