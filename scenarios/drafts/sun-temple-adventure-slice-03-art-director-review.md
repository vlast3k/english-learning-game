## Verdict

Conditional Go.

The reviewer found the core loop strong: find container, return to a familiar place, bring water back, visibly change the world, receive a lens. It is child-readable, map-driven, and prop-disciplined.

## Must-Fix Review Notes

1. Make the water cause fully visible: cup area -> continuous carved channel -> gate -> visible continuation toward courtyard.
2. Do not hide the Stone Lens behind an additional flower-inspection beat. The flower opens when water flows; the lens visibly glints inside it on courtyard arrival.
3. Give the cup-to-waterfall return a strong visual clue: one obvious empty cup and one calm, clear fillable pool edge.
4. Stage Sun Courtyard as the immediate next screen through the newly opened gate. Map travel may unlock but should not steal the moment.
5. Reconcile master campaign documentation with the live lens progression: Blue Lens placement reveals Temple Steps; Stone Lens placement reveals Mirror Hall.

## Optional Refinements

- Give the stone cup a small sun motif that matches the channel.
- Make the Stone Lens warm ochre/sandstone with a circular sun rim, clearly distinct from the Green and Blue lenses and not coin-like.
- Stage Temple Steps left-to-right: cup/channel foreground, closed gate middle, warm temple beyond.
- Give the stone sun flower the undisputed visual focus in Sun Courtyard.

## Integration

All must-fix notes are integrated into the slice-three plot, dependency graph, screen/prop map, curriculum ledger, campaign plot, and world map.

## Final Review State

Status: review integrated. The approved art and state rules are implemented; the final water effect follows the painted channel while preserving the fixed background arch.

## Asset-Plan Follow-Up

The later asset-and-prompt review refined the gate production plan: Temple Steps now uses a fixed open arch with fully opaque closed/open gate overlays, rather than a painted closed gate. See `sun-temple-adventure-slice-03-asset-review.md` for the current generation-ready requirements.
