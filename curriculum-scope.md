# English Lesson Scope For Game Planning

This document consolidates the English-learning source PDFs in `english-docs/` into a planning inventory for future game stages. It treats Blue Dot 1 as the grade-2 retention base, Blue Dot 2 Entry Reviews as the summer / next-step grammar target, and Cambridge Pre A1 Starters as an additional target vocabulary standard to merge into game content.

## Source Set

- `english-docs/comprehension-worksheets/`: Blue Dot 1 reading-comprehension worksheets, units 1-18.
- `english-docs/grammar-worksheets/`: Blue Dot 1 vocabulary and grammar worksheets, units 1-18.
- `english-docs/class-docs/bluedot1_spelling_bee.pdf`: Blue Dot 1 unit vocabulary.
- `english-docs/class-docs/bluedot2_entry_review_test_1.pdf` through `bluedot2_entry_review_test_9.pdf`: Blue Dot 2 entry review grammar and exercise types.
- `english-docs/506166-starters-movers-flyers-word-list-2025.pdf`: Cambridge Pre A1 Starters, A1 Movers, and A2 Flyers wordlists. Only the Pre A1 Starters list is merged here as a current target.

## Curriculum Goal

Every game level should exercise a mix of:

- Vocabulary recognition: picture-to-word, word-to-object, spoken/written clue-to-word.
- Sentence understanding: choose the sentence that matches the scene.
- Sentence production: reorder words, complete blanks, answer short questions.
- Reading comprehension: understand a short story, article, email, poem, profile, or dialogue.
- Grammar in context: use grammar to solve a game problem, not as isolated worksheet drilling.
- Spiral review: previously learned words and structures should recur in later stages.

## Core Grammar Constructs

### Blue Dot 1 Retention Grammar

| Area | Constructs | Example sentence patterns |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Verb be: statements | `I am`, `he/she/it is`, `you/we/they are`; short forms; negatives | `I'm seven years old.` `It isn't a burger.` `They aren't children.` |
| Verb be: identity questions | `What is it?`, `What are they?`, `Who is this?`, `Who are they?` | `What's this? It's a bike.` `Who are they? They're my friends.` |
| Existential statements | `There is/There's`, `There are`; singular/plural count nouns | `There's a bike.` `There are two girls.` |
| Place questions | `Where is/Where's`, `Where are`; answers with place phrases | `Where's the turtle? It's in the ocean.` |
| Prepositions of place | `in`, `on`, `under`, `next to`, `behind` | `The bee is under the flower.` `The rabbit is behind the plant.` |
| Simple present affirmatives | `I/you/we/they + verb`; `he/she/it + verb-s` | `We wear gloves.` `She drinks water.` |
| Simple present negatives | `I/you/we/they don't + verb`; `he/she/it doesn't + verb` | `I don't eat chicken.` `He doesn't make cookies.` |
| Simple present yes/no questions | `Do I/you/we/they + verb?`; `Does he/she/it + verb?` | `Do they play board games?` `Does she need food?` |
| Have: statements | `have`, `has`, `don't have`, `doesn't have` | `I have a sister.` `Helen doesn't have a hat.` |
| Demonstratives | `this`, `that`, `these`, `those` with singular/plural nouns | `This goat is young.` `Those goats are gray.` |
| Possessive adjectives | `my`, `your`, `his`, `her`, `our`, `their` | `Her name is Deniz.` `Our favorite subject is art.` |
| Possessive 's | person + `'s` | `It's Monica's feather.` `These are Tom's shells.` |
| Can and can't | ability questions and answers | `Can a dog run? Yes, it can.` `It can't fly.` |
| Present continuous statements | `am/is/are + verb-ing`; negatives | `She's brushing her teeth.` `They aren't watching TV.` |
| Present continuous questions | `Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?`; `What are you doing?` | `Is she sleeping?` `What are they looking at?` |
| Adverbs of frequency | `always`, `sometimes`, `never`; placement before main verb or after `be` | `She sometimes drinks milk.` `Our classroom is always neat.` |
| Word order | reorder statements and questions | `Does the farmer sell vegetables?` `They sometimes use the elevator.` |

## Exercise Type Inventory

Use these as game challenge templates:

- Picture choice: choose/check/circle the picture that matches a word or sentence.
- Word choice: circle the correct word from two or more options.
- Word completion: fill missing letters in a target word.
- Word search / hidden word: find vocabulary embedded in a letter string.
- Secret word: solve several picture clues to reveal a hidden word.
- Matching: match sentence halves, words to pictures, questions to answers, or problems to solutions.
- Sorting/categorizing: sort words by school/free time, people/subjects/shapes, country/city/both.
- Gap-fill with word bank: complete sentences, dialogues, or short texts.
- Grammar gap-fill: complete with `is/are`, `do/does`, `have/has`, `don't/doesn't`, `this/that/these/those`, possessives, or adverbs.
- Sentence correction: check/cross, then rewrite the correct sentence.
- Word order: reorder words into statements or questions.
- Short answers: answer yes/no or factual reading questions.
- True/false: identify whether a statement matches the text.
- Sequence: order events as beginning/middle/end or numbered story order.
- Cause/effect: match cause to effect.
- Compare/contrast: identify which character/place/animal a sentence describes.
- Context vocabulary: infer meaning of underlined words from a text and use them in a new sentence/dialogue.
- Personalization: complete sentences so they are true for the child.

## Game Stage Planning Implications

- Early stages should mix object collection with `be`, `what/who`, and basic vocabulary recognition.
- Campsite/jungle item interactions are ideal for prepositions: put objects `in`, `on`, `under`, `next to`, or `behind`.
- Animal encounters should carry `there is/are`, `where`, simple present, `can/can't`, and nature vocabulary.
- NPC dialogue should repeatedly ask `Who is this?`, `What are these?`, `Where are...?`, `Do you...?`, `Does it...?`.
- Inventory puzzles should use `have/has`, possession, and possessive adjectives: `Lina's rope`, `his map`, `our backpack`.
- Environmental missions should cover Units 15-17: seasons, animal movement, recycling, reuse, and present continuous.
- Reading-comprehension challenges should be short in-game artifacts: camp notes, guide emails, field-guide cards, poem stones, village signs, and story panels.
- Every new level should include a review pocket: one old grammar construct, one old vocabulary theme, one new construct/theme.

## Recommended Stage Sequence

1. Camp Introductions: identity, `be`, names, family, likes, `What/Who`.
2. Campsite Map: `there is/are`, prepositions, classroom/camp objects, animal spotting.
3. Village Friends: simple present with `I/you/we/they`, hobbies, communities, food.
4. Animal Trail: simple present with `he/she/it`, `Does...?`, animals, needs, habitats.
5. Inventory Rescue: `have/has`, possession, clothing, body, tools, object collection.
6. River And Mountain: `Where...?`, place phrases, transport, village/city/country comparison.
7. Festival Puzzle: clothes, music, New Year, possessive adjectives, people/culture.
8. Pattern Temple: shapes, numbers, demonstratives, sorting, sequences.
9. Seasons Garden: seasons, present continuous, nature changes, poem clues.
10. Earth Helper Mission: recycling/reuse, `can/can't`, present continuous questions, action verbs.
11. City-Country Finale: adverbs of frequency, emails/notes, all prior structures mixed.

Each stage should include at least one recognition challenge, one sentence-building challenge, one short reading artifact, and one optional review interaction.
